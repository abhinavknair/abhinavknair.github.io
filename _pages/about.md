I'm Abhinav K. Nair, an undergraduate Physics student currently pursuing B. Sc. (Honours) Physics. I have a great enthusiasm for physics, and I'm particularly interested in theoretical quantum mechanics and astrophysics. I have experience in computational quantum simulations, which include those of Schrödinger equation, Gross-Pitaevskii equation, and solitons. I have served as the Research Head of my department's Physics Society.


## Research Experience 
My undergraduate academic research works focus mainly on the theoretical aspects of the properties of Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC).

### BEC Collapse:
Conducted theoretical and computational study on the collapse of Bose-Einstein condensates under attractive interactions that describes a discrepancy in the experimentally obtained condition for condensate instability.

### Bright Soliton CNOT Gates:
Investigated the effectiveness of three-body interaction as an excellent parameter in controlling the properties of CNOT gates that can be realised using bright solitons in a BEC.

### Dissemination:
Presented these research findings at two international conferences, with the first study communicated to the Elsevier NIMB Proceedings journal for review.
